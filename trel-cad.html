TREL - Site Modeling

In Spring 2025, as the Mark 1 neared launch, I began working as a Structures Engineer for TREL’s Orbital Test Stand division, where we aim to create a launch and testing station at the J. J. Pickle Research Campus. My job was to create a realistic CAD of the hole at the site where the Orbital Test Stand would be built.

Summary: I created a CAD model of the site where the orbital test stand was to be placed, which I endearingly named the Hole. For this project, I was limited to a few photos and videos of the hole and rough dimensions for most of the parts.

Problem Statement: Create a life-size CAD model of the hole so the Structures teams could place their designed parts into the assembly for ease of integration. This CAD was also used extensively for presentations, including the Preliminary Design Review.

Project Timeline: Finish as quickly as possible since many other teams depended on my CAD to integrate their work.

Design Process: I decided to break down everything in the hole that I needed to CAD into a list, creating priorities to complete by specific presentations. First, I started with the overall CAD of the hole and the shed that was right on top of it. Next, I moved onto each floor and the stairs, making sure to model the metal grating pattern for realism.

Future Considerations: I really enjoyed this project since I was able to hone my modeling skills. While this process was enjoyable, I have a few considerations for the future. Initially, I wanted to place each part in the correct positions individually rather than using mates in the assembly, so if one piece was incorrect, another wouldn’t be too. However, this ended up being time-consuming to change. Additionally, due to complex geometries, the entire assembly took time to render, so I would likely either decrease details by lowering the metal grating quality or keep everything as a solid body.

Skills: CAD, Visualization
